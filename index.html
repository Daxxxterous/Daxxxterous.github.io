Everything about Taldykorgan

History of the city

  • It appeared in the XIX century as a Russian settlement, in the 2nd half of the XIX century - Gavrilovskoye, in 1921 it was renamed the village of Taldy—Kurgan. Since 1930 - the center of Talda-Kurgan district of Alma-Ata district of the Kazakh ASSR.
  • In 1944, it received the status of a city. In 1944-1959 and in 1967-1991 — the center of Talda-Kurgan region of the Kazakh SSR.
  • On May 4, 1993, by the Decree of the Presidium of the Supreme Council of Kazakhstan, the transcription of the name of the city of Taldy-Kurgan in Russian was changed to Taldykorgan. In 2001-2022 — the center of Almaty region.
  • Since 2022 — the center of the newly formed Zhetysu region. Today, about 145 thousand people, representing 70 peoples and nationalities, live in Taldykorgan.

Geographical location

It is located in the center of Semirechye on the banks of the Karatal River in the foothills of the Dzungarian Alatau at an altitude of over 602 m above sea level.

Population

As of July 1, 2022, the number of permanent residents of Taldykorgan is 145 365 people.

  1. Kazakhs — 128,652 people (74.97%)
  2. Russians — 33,824 people (19.53%
  3. Koreans — 4,236 people (2.45%)

Culture

The city has a drama theater named after Biken Rimova, the Almaty Regional Museum of Local Lore named after M. Tynyshpaev, the Ilyas Dzhansugurov Literary Museum, the Veterans Culture Park, Central, Zhastar, the S. Seifullin Library and cinemas. For physical culture and sports, the Zhetysu stadium with 5,000 seats, the Zhastar sports complex, 5 swimming pools, more than 30 gyms at enterprises and educational institutions work.
